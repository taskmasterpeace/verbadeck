## What is VerbaDeck?

VerbaDeck is an AI-powered presentation system that advances your slides automatically when you say specific trigger words. Instead of clicking through slides manually, you speak naturally during your presentation, and VerbaDeck listens for key phrases to move forward or backward through your content.

### Core Features

**Voice-Activated Navigation**
Speak naturally during your presentation. When you say a trigger word (like "audience" or "revenue"), VerbaDeck automatically advances to the next slide.

**AI-Powered Creation**
Answer a few questions about your topic, and AI generates a complete presentation with multiple slide options to choose from.

**Dual-Monitor Support**
Show a clean audience view on one screen while you see speaker notes, trigger words, and controls on your presenter screen.

**Flexible Content Processing**
Paste existing scripts, upload PowerPoint files, or create from scratch. VerbaDeck adapts to your workflow.

**Q&A Practice Mode**
Upload a knowledge base or resume and practice answering questions with voice-activated confirmation.

## Getting Started

### First Launch

When you open VerbaDeck, you'll see the home screen with three main paths:

1. **Create from Scratch** - AI helps you build a presentation from questions
2. **Process Existing Content** - Convert scripts or PowerPoint files
3. **Know It All Wall** - Practice Q&A sessions with voice confirmation

Choose the path that fits your needs. You can always switch between them later.

### The Workflow

VerbaDeck follows a simple 3-step workflow displayed at the top of your screen:

```
1. Create → 2. Edit → 3. Present
```

- **Create**: Generate or upload your content
- **Edit**: Customize slides, trigger words, and speaker notes
- **Present**: Deliver your presentation with voice control

The **Present** button stays disabled until you have content ready. This prevents accidentally starting an empty presentation.

## Creating Presentations from Scratch

### Overview

This path is perfect when you're starting fresh. AI acts as your presentation consultant, asking questions about your topic and generating professional slides based on your answers.

### Step-by-Step Process

#### 1. Choose Your Topic

Click **"Start from Scratch"** on the home screen.

AI will ask you questions like:
- What is your presentation about?
- Who is your target audience?
- What key points do you want to cover?
- What action do you want your audience to take?

**Example**: If you're presenting about "Sustainable Urban Farming":
- Topic: "How to start a rooftop garden in your city"
- Audience: "Urban apartment dwellers interested in growing food"
- Key points: "Benefits, getting started, maintenance tips"
- Call to action: "Join our community garden program"

#### 2. Answer the Questions

Type your answers naturally. You don't need to write perfect prose - AI will polish your ideas into professional content.

**Tip**: Be specific but conversational. Instead of "increase sales," say "increase sales by 30% in Q4 through targeted email campaigns."

#### 3. Review Generated Slides

AI generates multiple slide options for each section of your presentation. You'll see:

**Slide 1 Option A**
*Title: "Transform Your Balcony into a Garden"*
Content: Opening hook with statistics
Trigger word: "transform"

**Slide 1 Option B**
*Title: "Why Urban Farming Matters"*
Content: Problem-focused opening
Trigger word: "matters"

**Slide 1 Option C**
*Title: "Welcome to Green Living"*
Content: Personal story approach
Trigger word: "welcome"

#### 4. Choose Your Preferred Slides

Click on the slide option you prefer. You can:
- Choose different options for different sections
- Mix and match styles
- Preview how they flow together

#### 5. Add Speaker Notes (Optional)

For each slide, you can add speaker notes - reminders of what to say, statistics to mention, or stories to tell.

**Example Speaker Note**:
> "Start with story about Mrs. Johnson, 78, who grows 40 lbs of tomatoes on her balcony. Mention the 2023 urban farming study showing 60% of participants saved $200/month on groceries."

#### 6. Customize Trigger Words

Each slide has a default trigger word. You can change it to whatever feels natural in your speaking style.

**Default**: "transform"
**Alternative options**: "change", "start", "begin"

You can also add multiple trigger words to the same slide. For example, if slide 2 should advance when you say "benefits" OR "advantages" OR "why", add all three.

## Processing Existing Content

### Overview

Already have a script, speech, or PowerPoint presentation? VerbaDeck can convert it into a voice-activated presentation automatically.

### From Text Scripts

#### 1. Access the Processor

Click **"Process Content"** on the home screen.

#### 2. Paste Your Script

Copy and paste your existing content. VerbaDeck accepts:
- Word documents (paste the text)
- Speech scripts
- Presentation outlines
- Training materials
- Blog posts or articles

**No formatting needed** - just paste the raw text.

#### 3. AI Splits into Sections

VerbaDeck's AI analyzes your content and:
- Identifies natural section breaks
- Creates slide divisions
- Suggests trigger words for each section
- Generates speaker notes

**Example**: If you paste a 2000-word product pitch, AI might split it into:
- Introduction (trigger: "today")
- Problem statement (trigger: "challenge")
- Solution overview (trigger: "solution")
- Key features (trigger: "features")
- Pricing (trigger: "invest")
- Call to action (trigger: "started")

#### 4. Review and Adjust

Check the auto-generated sections. You can:
- Merge sections that are too short
- Split sections that are too long
- Change trigger words
- Edit the content

#### 5. Add Images (Optional)

Upload images for each slide or let AI suggest image prompts based on your content.

### From PowerPoint Files

#### 1. Upload Your File

Click **"Upload PowerPoint"** in the Process Content view.

Supported formats:
- .pptx (PowerPoint 2010 and later)
- .ppt (older PowerPoint files)

**File size limit**: 50MB

#### 2. Conversion Process

VerbaDeck extracts:
- Text from each slide
- Speaker notes (if present)
- Images (embedded photos, diagrams)
- Slide order

#### 3. Assign Trigger Words

Since PowerPoint doesn't have trigger words, AI suggests them based on:
- Slide titles
- First sentence of each slide
- Key words in the content

Review and adjust as needed.

#### 4. Test the Flow

Use the preview feature to see how your converted presentation flows. Make sure trigger words feel natural when spoken aloud.

## Know It All Wall (Q&A Practice)

### Overview

Know It All Wall is a unique feature for practicing Q&A sessions, interviews, or presentations where you need to answer unpredictable questions. Upload your knowledge base, and VerbaDeck generates practice questions with voice-activated confirmation.

### Perfect For

- Job interview preparation
- Conference Q&A practice
- Customer demo sessions
- Teaching and training
- Expert panel preparation

### How It Works

#### 1. Upload Your Knowledge Base

Click **"Start Q&A Practice"** on the home screen.

Upload one of these document types:
- Resume or CV
- Product documentation
- Company overview
- Research paper
- Course materials
- FAQ document

**Supported formats**: PDF, Word, plain text

#### 2. AI Generates Questions

Based on your knowledge base, AI creates:
- Common questions people might ask
- Difficult or challenging questions
- Clarifying questions
- Follow-up questions

**Example** (Resume upload):
- "Tell me about your experience with project management."
- "Why did you leave your previous role?"
- "What's your biggest professional accomplishment?"
- "How do you handle tight deadlines?"

#### 3. Practice Answering

Questions appear on screen one at a time. Answer them out loud as if you're in the actual situation.

#### 4. Voice-Activated Confirmation

After answering, say the **keyword** shown on screen to confirm you're done and move to the next question.

**Example flow**:
```
Question: "What's your experience with leadership?"

[You answer for 60 seconds]

Say "NEXT" to continue
```

When you say "next," VerbaDeck moves to the next question.

#### 5. Alternative Keywords

You can customize the confirmation keyword to anything comfortable:
- "done"
- "continue"
- "go"
- "next question"

#### 6. Practice Modes

**Timed Mode**: Set a time limit for each answer (30 seconds, 60 seconds, 90 seconds).

**Surprise Mode**: Questions appear in random order so you can't memorize the sequence.

**Difficult First**: Hardest questions appear first to build confidence.

### Using Know It All Wall for Interviews

#### Before Your Interview

1. Upload your resume 3-5 days before the interview
2. Practice 15-20 minutes daily
3. Record yourself (use your phone) to hear how you sound
4. Refine answers based on what feels awkward

#### During Practice

- Stand up if you'll be standing during the real event
- Wear interview clothes to build comfort
- Practice in a quiet room with good audio
- Speak at your natural pace - don't rush

#### Common Mistakes

❌ **Don't**: Memorize word-for-word answers
✅ **Do**: Practice key points and natural phrasing

❌ **Don't**: Skip questions you find difficult
✅ **Do**: Practice difficult questions 3x more than easy ones

❌ **Don't**: Practice sitting if you'll be standing
✅ **Do**: Match your practice environment to the real situation

## Editing Your Presentation

### Accessing the Editor

After creating content (via any of the three paths), click **"2. Edit"** in the workflow bar at the top. You'll see a count like "Edit (7)" showing how many slides you have.

### The Editor Interface

The editor shows your presentation in a vertical list:

```
Section 1: Introduction
  Content: [Welcome to today's presentation...]
  Trigger: "audience"
  Speaker Notes: [Start with eye contact...]

Section 2: Problem Statement
  Content: [Many businesses struggle with...]
  Trigger: "challenge"
  Speaker Notes: [Cite the McKinsey study...]
```

### Editing Content

#### Change Slide Text

Click on any section's content to edit it. You have a rich text editor with:
- Bold, italic, underline
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Headings and subheadings
- Links

**Best practice**: Keep slides concise. If a section has more than 100 words, consider splitting it.

#### Reorder Slides

Drag and drop sections to change the order. Grab the handle (⋮⋮) on the left side of each section.

#### Add or Remove Slides

**Add**: Click "+ Add Section" between any two existing sections or at the end.

**Remove**: Click the trash icon (🗑️) on any section. You'll be asked to confirm deletion.

#### Merge Sections

Select two adjacent sections and click "Merge" to combine them into one slide with both trigger words available.

### Editing Trigger Words

#### Change the Primary Trigger

Click on the trigger word shown for a section. A dropdown appears with:
- AI-suggested alternatives
- Recently used triggers
- Custom entry field

Type your own trigger word if the suggestions don't fit.

**Rules for trigger words**:
- Must be at least 4 characters
- Should be unique (not used in other sections)
- Should be easy to pronounce
- Should feel natural in context

#### Add Alternative Triggers

Click "+ Add Trigger" below the primary trigger. You can add up to 5 alternative triggers per section.

**Example**: Section about revenue might have triggers:
- Primary: "revenue"
- Alternatives: "sales", "income", "earnings"

When you say ANY of these words, the slide advances.

#### Test Trigger Words

Click "Test Triggers" to see all trigger words highlighted in yellow throughout your content. This helps catch accidental triggers - words that appear in the wrong sections.

**Warning**: If "revenue" is a trigger for Section 5, but it also appears in Section 2's content, you might accidentally skip ahead. Fix this by choosing a more unique trigger or removing the word from earlier sections.

### Editing Speaker Notes

#### Add Speaker Notes

Click "+ Add Notes" under any section. Speaker notes are only visible to you on the presenter screen - audiences never see them.

**Good speaker notes include**:
- Key statistics with sources
- Stories or examples to mention
- Timing reminders ("spend 2 minutes here")
- Questions to ask the audience
- Transitions to the next section

**Example**:
> "Ask audience: 'How many of you have experienced this problem?' Wait for hands. Mention that 73% of companies in the industry face this (cite Forrester 2024 report). Transition with: 'That's exactly why we developed...'"

#### Format Speaker Notes

Use markdown formatting:
- **Bold** for emphasis
- *Italic* for side notes
- `Code blocks` for exact quotes
- Bullet points for lists

### Adding Images

#### Upload Images

Each section can have one main image. Click "Upload Image" or drag and drop a file.

**Supported formats**: JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP
**Recommended size**: 1920x1080px for best quality
**Maximum file size**: 10MB per image

#### AI-Generated Images

Don't have images? Click "Generate Image" and VerbaDeck will:
1. Analyze your section content
2. Create a descriptive prompt
3. Generate an image using AI
4. Let you regenerate if you don't like it

You can edit the prompt before generating. Be specific:

❌ **Vague**: "business meeting"
✅ **Specific**: "diverse team of professionals brainstorming around a modern conference table, large windows showing city skyline, natural lighting, professional corporate environment"

#### Image Positioning

Choose how images appear:
- **Full Screen**: Image fills the entire slide
- **Left Half**: Image on left, text on right (50/50 split)
- **Right Half**: Image on right, text on left (50/50 split)
- **Background**: Image behind text (text appears in overlay box)

### Customizing Slide Layouts

#### Layout Options

Each section can use a different layout:

**Title Slide**
- Large centered title
- Subtitle below
- No image or background image only

**Content Slide**
- Title at top
- Bullet points or paragraphs
- Optional image on left or right

**Image Focus**
- Large image (75% of screen)
- Small text overlay or caption

**50/50 Split**
- Equal space for image and text
- Best for detailed content with supporting visuals

**Quote Slide**
- Large centered quote
- Attribution below
- Minimal design

#### Applying Layouts

Click the layout dropdown at the top of each section and choose from the 5 options. Preview updates immediately.

**Tip**: Use varied layouts to maintain visual interest. Don't use the same layout for more than 3 consecutive slides.

### Preview Mode

Click "Preview Presentation" to see how your slides will look during the actual presentation. This shows:
- The audience view (what people see on screen)
- Transition effects between slides
- Image positioning and text readability

Use preview to:
- Check that text is readable from a distance
- Verify images aren't pixelated
- Ensure color contrast is sufficient
- Test the overall flow

Press ESC to exit preview mode.

## Presenting with Voice Control

### Starting Your Presentation

#### 1. Prepare Your Environment

Before presenting:

**Audio Setup**
- Test your microphone (click "🎤 Voice" button to check levels)
- Close browser tabs playing audio
- Disable desktop notifications
- Set volume to 70-80% (not max, to avoid feedback)

**Display Setup**
- Connect external monitor if using dual screens
- Test that both screens show content
- Set presenter screen as your laptop, audience screen as external monitor

**Browser Setup**
- Use Chrome or Edge for best performance (Safari works but is slower)
- Close other applications to free up memory
- Disable browser extensions that might interfere

#### 2. Click "3. Present"

The Present button becomes active once you have at least one slide. Click it to enter presentation mode.

You'll see the presenter view with:
- Current slide (large, centered)
- Speaker notes (below slide)
- Trigger words (highlighted in yellow)
- Next slide preview (bottom right)
- Voice control status (top right)

#### 3. Start Voice Control

Click the **"🎤 Voice"** button in the top right corner. It turns from gray to blue when active.

**Permission Required**: Your browser will ask permission to use your microphone. Click "Allow."

**Status Indicator**: A small "Listening" badge appears when voice control is active.

### During Your Presentation

#### How Voice Advancement Works

As you speak naturally, VerbaDeck listens for trigger words. When it hears one:

1. ✨ The trigger word briefly flashes yellow on screen
2. 🎯 The slide advances to the next section
3. 🔊 You hear a subtle "swoosh" sound (optional, can be disabled)
4. ➡️ The next slide appears

**Important**: There's a 2-second "cooldown" after each advance to prevent accidental double-triggers if you say the same word twice quickly.

#### Speaking Naturally

**Do's:**
- ✅ Speak at your normal pace
- ✅ Use natural inflection and emphasis
- ✅ Pause between ideas
- ✅ Make eye contact with audience (not the screen)

**Don'ts:**
- ❌ Don't shout trigger words
- ❌ Don't pause awkwardly before trigger words
- ❌ Don't slow down unnaturally
- ❌ Don't stare at the screen waiting for advancement

**Pro tip**: Treat trigger words like any other word in your sentence. If "revenue" is a trigger and your sentence is "This increased our revenue by 40%," just say it normally. VerbaDeck will catch it.

#### Going Back

Said the wrong trigger word? No problem.

Say **"BACK"** or **"GO BACK"** to return to the previous slide.

You can also say:
- "previous"
- "return"
- "last slide"

#### Pausing Voice Control

Need to pause for Q&A or a demonstration?

Click the **"🎤 Voice"** button again to disable voice control temporarily. The button turns gray and the "Listening" badge disappears.

Click again to resume when ready.

**Use case**: During a product demo, pause voice control so you can say trigger words naturally without advancing slides.

#### Manual Control

Even with voice control active, you can manually advance using:
- **Spacebar**: Next slide
- **Left arrow**: Previous slide
- **Right arrow**: Next slide
- **Number keys (1-9)**: Jump to slide 1-9
- **Home**: Jump to first slide
- **End**: Jump to last slide

### Live Transcript

At the bottom of the presenter screen, you'll see a live transcript of what VerbaDeck hears you saying.

**Why it's useful**:
- Confirms microphone is working
- Shows what trigger words were detected
- Helps you see if you're speaking clearly enough
- Records your presentation for later review

**Privacy note**: Transcripts are only stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to external servers.

### Managing Technical Issues During Presentation

#### Microphone Not Working

**Symptoms**: "Listening" badge shows, but transcript is empty.

**Quick fixes**:
1. Click Voice button off, then on again
2. Check microphone isn't muted at system level
3. Try refreshing the page (your progress is saved)
4. Switch to manual control (spacebar) as backup

#### Trigger Words Not Advancing

**Symptoms**: Transcript shows your speech, but slides don't advance.

**Possible causes**:
- You're saying a word similar but not exact (e.g., "challenge" vs "challenges")
- Background noise is interfering
- You're within the 2-second cooldown period

**Quick fix**: Switch to manual control and continue.

#### Slides Advancing Too Fast

**Symptoms**: Slides skip ahead unexpectedly.

**Possible causes**:
- Multiple trigger words too close together in your speech
- Background conversation being picked up
- Echo from speakers feeding back into microphone

**Quick fixes**:
1. Pause voice control immediately
2. Say "back" multiple times to return to correct slide
3. Continue with manual control
4. Lower speaker volume to prevent feedback

## Presenter View

### Overview

The presenter view is what YOU see on your laptop screen during a presentation. It includes tools and information that help you deliver effectively, while the audience sees a clean, distraction-free view.

### Screen Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Voice Status │ Section 3 of 12 │ Timer: 00:15:30 │ Settings │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                           │
│              [Current Slide Display]                      │
│                                                           │
│  This slide shows quarterly revenue growth charts        │
│  with specific numbers for each region highlighted       │
│                                                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Speaker Notes:                                           │
│  • Mention APAC grew 45% (highest)                       │
│  • North America steady at 12%                           │
│  • Ask "What do you think drove APAC growth?"           │
│  • Trigger word: "revenue"                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Next Up:  [Small preview of next slide]                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Elements

#### 1. Status Bar (Top)

**Voice Status**: Shows if voice control is active
- 🎤 Blue = Listening
- 🎤 Gray = Paused
- Click to toggle

**Progress Indicator**: "Section 3 of 12"
- Shows where you are in the presentation
- Helps pace yourself

**Timer**: Elapsed time since presentation started
- Resets when you restart
- Useful for staying on schedule
- Format: HH:MM:SS

**Settings Button**: Quick access to adjust presentation settings without exiting

#### 2. Current Slide (Center)

Large display of the active slide exactly as the audience sees it. What you see here is what they see on their screen.

#### 3. Speaker Notes (Middle Bottom)

Your private notes for this slide. These include:
- Key points to mention
- Statistics and data
- Trigger word reminder
- Timing suggestions
- Transitions to next slide

**Formatting**: Speaker notes support bold, italic, and bullet points for easy scanning.

#### 4. Next Slide Preview (Bottom Right)

Small preview of the next slide. This helps you:
- Prepare smooth transitions
- See what's coming up
- Anticipate audience questions
- Time your current section appropriately

**Example transition**: If your current slide ends on a problem and the next slide shows a solution, you can say "So how do we solve this? Let me show you..." right before advancing.

### Presenter-Only Features

#### Keyboard Shortcuts

| Key | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Spacebar** | Next slide (even with voice control) |
| **Left Arrow** | Previous slide |
| **Right Arrow** | Next slide |
| **B** | Blackout screen (audience sees black, you see presenter view) |
| **W** | Whiteout screen (audience sees white) |
| **P** | Pause/Resume voice control |
| **ESC** | Exit presentation mode |
| **H** | Show help overlay |
| **1-9** | Jump to slide 1-9 |

**Use case for blackout**: During Q&A or breaks, press B to show a black screen instead of leaving a slide visible. This keeps audience attention on you, not the screen.

#### On-Screen Timer

The timer helps you pace your presentation.

**Time management tips**:
- If your presentation should be 20 minutes, check the timer when you reach your halfway point (slide 6 of 12). You should be around 10 minutes.
- Set a mental "checkpoint" - e.g., "I should be at slide 8 by minute 15"
- If you're running over, identify sections you can shorten on the fly

#### Section Indicators

The "Section X of Y" display does more than just count. Pay attention to:
- **Early sections (1-3)**: Take your time, build rapport with audience
- **Middle sections (4-8)**: Core content, stay on message
- **Final sections (9-12)**: Wrap up, call to action, don't rush the close

### Presenter View Tips

#### Multi-Monitor Setup

**Recommended**: Use a laptop + external monitor/projector

**Configuration**:
1. Your laptop screen = Presenter View
2. External monitor = Audience View

**Setting it up**:
- Open VerbaDeck on your laptop
- Press "3. Present"
- Click "Audience View" in top right
- Drag that window to the external monitor
- Press F11 to make it full screen

**Check before presenting**:
- Can you see both screens from your presenting position?
- Is your laptop angle comfortable?
- Is the external monitor visible to the entire audience?

#### Single Screen Presenting

Don't have two screens? You can still present effectively.

**Option 1: Presenter View Only**
- Present using your laptop screen
- Audience watches your laptop (small audience only)
- You see speaker notes

**Option 2: Audience View on Single Screen**
- Click "Audience View" when presenting
- You and audience see the same clean view
- No speaker notes visible
- Use printed notes as backup
- Voice control still works

#### Using Speaker Notes Effectively

**Before the presentation**:
- Print your speaker notes as backup
- Highlight key statistics and names
- Mark sections where you want to pause for questions

**During the presentation**:
- Glance at notes briefly
- Don't read them word-for-word
- Use them as memory triggers, not scripts

**If you lose your place**:
- The trigger word reminder helps you know where to steer your speech
- Next slide preview shows what's coming
- Take a brief pause, glance at notes, continue smoothly

## Audience View

### Overview

The audience view is a clean, distraction-free display optimized for large screens and projectors. It contains ONLY the content your audience should see - no controls, no notes, no clutter.

### What Audiences See

```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                             │
│                                             │
│        Quarterly Revenue Growth            │
│                                             │
│        [Large, clear chart or image]       │
│                                             │
│        • APAC: +45%                        │
│        • North America: +12%               │
│        • Europe: +8%                       │
│                                             │
│                                             │
└───────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**That's it.** No buttons, no menus, no distractions. Pure content.

### Opening Audience View

#### Method 1: Automatic (Dual Monitors)

If you have two screens connected:

1. Click "3. Present"
2. VerbaDeck detects two monitors
3. Asks: "Open Audience View on external monitor?"
4. Click "Yes"
5. Audience View automatically opens full-screen on external monitor

#### Method 2: Manual

1. Click "3. Present" to enter Presenter View
2. Click "Audience View" button in top right corner
3. A new window opens
4. Drag this window to your external monitor/projector
5. Press F11 to make it full screen
6. Return focus to Presenter View window

#### Method 3: Direct Link

Share this URL directly: `http://localhost:5173/audience`

Useful for:
- Remote presentations (send link to remote participants)
- Multiple displays (open on several screens)
- Recording (point screen recorder at this view)

### Audience View Features

#### Synchronized Display

Audience View stays perfectly synchronized with your Presenter View. When you:
- Advance slides → Audience View advances
- Go back → Audience View goes back
- Jump to slide 5 → Audience View jumps to slide 5

**No delay**: Changes appear instantly (under 50ms lag).

#### Optimized Layouts

Audience View automatically adjusts layouts for optimal viewing:

**Text-Only Slides**
- Centered content
- Large, readable font (min 28pt)
- Plenty of white space

**Image Slides**
- Full-screen images
- Text overlays (if included) with high contrast
- Captions in easy-to-read positions

**50/50 Splits**
- Image on one side (full height)
- Text on other side (proper spacing)
- Automatic scaling to fill screen

#### Dark Mode Support

If audience is in a dimly lit room (conference, theater, auditorium), Audience View automatically switches to dark mode:
- Black background
- White or light text
- Reduced blue light
- Better for long presentations

**Manual control**: Click the 🌙 icon in Audience View to toggle dark/light mode.

### Remote Audience

Want people to follow along on their own devices?

#### Sharing Audience View

**Option 1: Local Network**
1. Get your computer's local IP address
2. Share: `http://[YOUR_IP]:5173/audience`
3. Anyone on same WiFi can view

**Example**: `http://192.168.1.5:5173/audience`

**Option 2: Screen Sharing (Zoom, Teams, etc.)**
1. Open Audience View in full screen
2. Share that window/screen in your video call
3. Remote participants see the clean Audience View
4. You keep Presenter View on your other screen

**Option 3: Recording**
1. Use screen recording software (OBS, Camtasia)
2. Record the Audience View screen
3. Post-edit video later
4. You get clean recordings without your notes visible

### Audience View Best Practices

#### Before Presenting

## Best Practices

### Choosing Trigger Words

#### What Makes a Good Trigger Word

**Ideal characteristics**:
- ✅ Unique to that section (doesn't appear elsewhere)
- ✅ Easy to pronounce
- ✅ Feels natural to say in context
- ✅ 4-10 letters long
- ✅ Single word (not phrases)

**Examples of great trigger words**:
- "revenue"
- "customers"
- "solution"
- "results"
- "challenge"

**Examples of poor trigger words**:
- "it" (too common)
- "the" (too common)
- "remuneratio" (too hard to say)
- "and then" (phrase, not word)

#### Testing Trigger Words

**Read-aloud test**:
1. Read your slide content out loud
2. Does the trigger word appear naturally in your speech?
3. Can you say the section without saying the trigger word multiple times?

**Uniqueness test**:
1. Use the "Find" feature (Ctrl+F) in editor
2. Search for your trigger word
3. Does it appear in other sections? If yes, choose a different trigger.

**Pronunciation test**:
1. Say the trigger word 5 times fast
2. If you stumble, it's not a good trigger word
3. Test with friends/colleagues

#### Common Trigger Word Pitfalls

**Problem 1: Too Similar**
- "customer" in slide 2, "customers" in slide 5
- VerbaDeck treats these as same word (plural detection)
- Solution: Use "client" for one, "customer" for other

**Problem 2: Filler Words**
- "okay", "so", "now", "next"
- You might say these accidentally
- Solution: Choose content-specific words

**Problem 3: One-Letter Difference**
- "coast" and "cost"
- Voice recognition might confuse them
- Solution: Choose words that sound very different

### Structuring Your Presentation

#### The Rule of Threes

Human brains remember things in groups of three. Structure slides in threes:

**Three main points**:
1. Problem
2. Solution
3. Results

**Three benefits**:
1. Saves time
2. Reduces costs
3. Improves quality

**Three steps**:
1. Sign up
2. Configure
3. Launch

#### Opening Strong

**First slide should**:
- Hook attention with surprising fact or question
- Clearly state what presentation is about
- Show what's in it for the audience

**Example opening slides**:

❌ **Weak**: "Welcome to my presentation on cloud computing"

✅ **Strong**: "73% of companies that don't adopt cloud computing fail within 3 years. Here's how to make sure you're not one of them."

#### Middle Content

**Keep slides focused**:
- One main idea per slide
- Max 5 bullet points per slide
- Max 50 words per slide (unless it's a quote)

**Use variety**:
- Text slides: For concepts
- Image slides: For emotional impact
- Chart slides: For data
- Quote slides: For authority

**Don't**:
- Put 3 different topics on one slide
- Use slides as your speaker notes (you have actual speaker notes for that)
- Read slides word-for-word

#### Closing Strong

**Final slide should**:
- Summarize key takeaway (one sentence)
- Give clear next step
- Provide contact info

**Example closing slides**:

❌ **Weak**: "Thank you! Questions?"

✅ **Strong**: "Start your 30-day free trial today → www.yoursite.com | Questions? email: you@company.com"

- Go through entire presentation 3 times

- After asking audience a question

### Design Best Practices

#### Visual Hierarchy

**Size matters**:
- Titles: 72pt
- Subtitles: 48pt
- Body text: 36pt
- Captions: 28pt

**Contrast matters**:
- Dark text on light background (or vice versa)
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Test readability from back of room

**Consistency matters**:
- Same font throughout presentation
- Same color scheme (max 3 colors)
- Same slide layout style

#### Using Images

**Image quality**:
- Minimum 1920x1080px resolution
- Avoid pixelated or blurry images
- Don't stretch images (maintains aspect ratio)

**Image relevance**:
- Images should support content, not distract
- Don't use clip art (looks unprofessional)
- Use authentic photos over stock photos when possible

**Image licensing**:
- Only use images you have rights to
- AI-generated images in VerbaDeck are licensed for your use
- Credit sources when appropriate

#### Color Psychology

**Blue**: Trust, professionalism, stability (good for business)
**Green**: Growth, success, money (good for finance)
**Red**: Urgency, passion, excitement (use sparingly)
**Orange**: Energy, creativity, friendliness (good for startups)
**Purple**: Luxury, wisdom, creativity (good for premium brands)
**Black**: Power, sophistication, elegance (good for high-end)

**Pro tip**: Pick one primary color and use variations of it, plus white/gray.

## Troubleshooting

### Voice Control Issues

#### Microphone Not Detected

**Symptoms**: Voice button stays gray, can't activate voice control.

**Solutions**:
1. **Check browser permissions**
   - Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Site Settings → Microphone → Allow
   - Safari: Preferences → Websites → Microphone → Allow
   - Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Microphone → Allow

2. **Check system microphone**
   - Windows: Settings → System → Sound → Input device
   - Mac: System Preferences → Sound → Input → Select microphone

3. **Restart browser**
   - Close all browser windows
   - Reopen and load VerbaDeck
   - Try again

4. **Try different browser**
   - Chrome works best
   - Edge is second best
   - Safari works but slower
   - Firefox has occasional issues

#### Trigger Words Not Working

**Symptom**: Transcript shows your speech, but slides don't advance when you say trigger words.

**Diagnosis**:
1. Look at live transcript - do you see the exact trigger word appear?
2. If yes but slide didn't advance, check spelling in editor
3. If no, you might be mispronouncing or saying a similar word

**Solutions**:
1. **Speak more clearly**
   - Enunciate trigger words
   - Don't mumble or rush
   - Pause slightly after trigger word

2. **Check trigger word spelling**
   - Open editor
   - Verify exact spelling matches what you're saying
   - Check for typos

3. **Change trigger word**
   - Pick a word that's easier to pronounce
   - Choose a more distinct word
   - Test new trigger in voice control

4. **Adjust microphone sensitivity**
   - Settings → Voice Control → Sensitivity → High
   - Test if trigger now works

#### False Triggering

**Symptom**: Slides advance when you didn't say the trigger word.

**Common causes**:
- Background conversation picked up by microphone
- Similar-sounding word triggered detection
- Someone else speaking nearby

**Solutions**:
1. **Reduce sensitivity**
   - Settings → Voice Control → Sensitivity → Low
   - Reduces pickup of quiet sounds

2. **Increase cooldown**
   - Settings → Voice Control → Cooldown → 3 seconds
   - Prevents rapid double-triggers

3. **Choose distinct triggers**
   - Avoid common words
   - Pick words that sound unique
   - Test with "Find" function to ensure uniqueness

4. **Control environment**
   - Present in quieter location
   - Ask audience to hold questions until end
   - Mute microphone during audience participation

#### Transcript Not Appearing

**Symptom**: Voice control is active (blue button), but transcript area stays empty.

**Solutions**:
1. **Check audio input**
   - Speak louder
   - Move microphone closer
   - Test microphone in system settings

2. **Browser permissions**
   - Reload page
   - Click "Allow" on microphone permission prompt
   - Check site permissions didn't get blocked

3. **Network issue**
   - Voice transcription requires internet
   - Check internet connection
   - Try reloading page

4. **Fallback**
   - Use manual control (spacebar)
   - Continue presentation without voice control
   - Fix after presentation

### Display Issues

#### Audience View Not Updating

**Symptom**: You advance in Presenter View, but Audience View doesn't change.

**Solutions**:
1. **Refresh Audience View**
   - Click in Audience View window
   - Press F5 to refresh
   - Should re-sync automatically

2. **Reopen Audience View**
   - Close Audience View window
   - Click "Audience View" button again
   - Drag to external monitor

3. **Check connection**
   - If using remote Audience View (over network)
   - Check WiFi connection
   - Reload the URL

4. **Restart presentation**
   - Exit Presenter View (ESC)
   - Click "3. Present" again
   - Reopen Audience View

#### Slides Look Blurry on Projector

**Solutions**:
1. **Check projector resolution**
   - Set projector to native resolution (usually 1920x1080)
   - Windows: Display settings → Resolution
   - Mac: System Preferences → Displays → Resolution

2. **Use higher-resolution images**
   - Replace images with higher-res versions
   - Minimum 1920x1080 for full-screen images
   - 4K images (3840x2160) for best quality

3. **Adjust font sizes**
   - Increase font size in Settings
   - Make text larger in editor
   - Test readability from back of room

4. **Check cable connection**
   - Use HDMI instead of VGA if possible
   - Ensure cable is fully connected
   - Try different cable

#### External Monitor Not Detected

**Windows Solutions**:
1. Press **Windows Key + P**
2. Select "Extend"
3. Check if monitor appears

If not:
- Right-click Desktop → Display Settings
- Click "Detect"
- Select "Multiple displays" → "Extend"

**Mac Solutions**:
1. System Preferences → Displays
2. Click "Detect Displays"
3. Arrangement → Uncheck "Mirror Displays"

**Physical checks**:
- Try different video cable
- Ensure monitor is powered on
- Check cable is in correct port

### Performance Issues

#### Slow Response / Lag

**Symptoms**: Slides take 2-3 seconds to advance, voice control lags.

**Solutions**:
1. **Close other browser tabs**
   - VerbaDeck needs memory
   - Close tabs playing video/audio
   - Close other applications

2. **Disable slide transitions**
   - Settings → Display → Transitions → None
   - Instant transitions = no animation lag

3. **Reduce image sizes**
   - Use compressed images
   - Don't use 20MB images
   - 2MB per image is plenty

4. **Clear browser cache**
   - Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data
   - Refresh VerbaDeck

5. **Use better browser**
   - Chrome performs best
   - Update to latest version
   - Restart browser

#### Audio Cuts Out

**Symptom**: Voice control works intermittently, transcript stops and starts.

**Solutions**:
1. **Check microphone connection**
   - Unplug and replug USB microphone
   - Check Bluetooth microphone battery
   - Use wired microphone instead

2. **Reduce CPU usage**
   - Close background applications
   - Close other browser tabs
   - Disable antivirus during presentation

3. **Change audio sample rate**
   - Windows: Sound settings → Device properties → Advanced
   - Set to 16-bit, 16000 Hz
   - Restart browser

### Content Issues

#### Lost Presentation

**Symptom**: Presentation disappeared from Library, auto-save didn't work.

**Recovery steps**:
1. **Check auto-saves**
   - Library → "Restore from Auto-save"
   - Shows all auto-saves from past 7 days
   - Load most recent

2. **Check different browser**
   - Did you create it in Chrome but now using Edge?
   - Presentations save per-browser
   - Open the original browser

3. **Check exported files**
   - Did you export to .vdeck?
   - Check Downloads folder
   - Load from file

**Prevention**:
- Always manually save important presentations
- Export to .vdeck file regularly
- Email backup to yourself before big presentations

#### Trigger Word Appears in Wrong Slide

**Symptom**: The word "revenue" is a trigger for slide 5, but it appears in slide 3's content, making you skip ahead.

**Solutions**:
1. **Change trigger word**
   - Pick a word that doesn't appear in earlier slides
   - Use "Find" function to check

2. **Rewrite earlier content**
   - Remove the word from earlier slides
   - Use synonym instead
   - Example: Change "revenue" to "income" in slide 3

3. **Add multiple triggers**
   - If slide 5 needs "revenue" as trigger
   - Add alternative triggers like "sales", "income"
   - You can say any of them to advance

4. **Reorder slides**
   - Move slides so problem word appears before it's a trigger
   - Ensures you say it in correct order

#### Images Won't Upload

**Symptom**: Click "Upload Image", select file, nothing happens.

**Solutions**:
1. **Check file size**
   - Maximum 10MB per image
   - Compress large images
   - Use online tools like TinyPNG

2. **Check file format**
   - Use JPG, PNG, GIF, or WebP
   - Don't use PSD, AI, or other formats
   - Convert if necessary

3. **Check permissions**
   - Browser might be blocking file access
   - Reload page and try again

4. **Use image URL instead**
   - Upload image to Imgur or similar
   - Click "Add Image from URL"
   - Paste URL

### Collaboration Issues

#### Sharing with Team

**Symptom**: Team member can't open .vdeck file you sent.

**Solutions**:
1. **Check VerbaDeck version**
   - Both people need compatible versions
   - Update to latest version

2. **Export correctly**
   - Library → Select presentation → "Export"
   - Send the .vdeck file (not screenshot)

3. **Try alternative format**
   - Export as PowerPoint
   - They can open in PowerPoint
   - Then import into VerbaDeck

4. **Use cloud sync**
   - Settings → Cloud Sync
   - Share cloud link instead
   - They access via browser

**Murphy's Law Plan**

If things go wrong:

**Scenario 1: Voice control fails**
→ Use spacebar for manual control. Audience won't know the difference.

**Scenario 2: Laptop dies**
→ Pull out printed notes, present without slides. Better than canceling.

**Scenario 3: Projector fails**
→ Share screen via Zoom/Teams to audience laptops. Or present without visuals.

**Scenario 4: Internet dies**
→ VerbaDeck works offline (voice control requires internet, but manual control doesn't).

❌ **Written**: "Utilize"
✅ **Spoken**: "Use"

❌ **Written**: "In order to"

Can your grandmother understand your presentation? If yes, you've achieved clarity.

Imagine explaining your presentation to someone at a party. That conversational tone? Use it in your actual presentation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use VerbaDeck offline?**

A: Partially. You can create, edit, and present using manual control (spacebar) offline. Voice control requires internet connection for speech recognition.

**Q: How many presentations can I save?**

A: Unlimited in your browser's Library. Storage limit is your hard drive space.

**Q: Can multiple presenters use the same laptop?**

A: Yes. Each person creates presentations in their browser profile, or you share files via Export/Import.

**Q: Does it work with teleprompters?**

A: Yes! VerbaDeck can send Presenter View to a teleprompter display.

**Q: Can I use it for webinars?**

A: Yes. Share Audience View via Zoom/Teams screen share. You see Presenter View locally.

**Q: What if I speak multiple languages during presentation?**

A: Voice control works best with single language. For multi-language presentations, use manual control or set trigger words in the language you're currently speaking.

**Q: Can I customize the Audience View design?**

A: Settings → Display → Audience View Font and colors. Full theme customization coming in future version.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**

A: Not yet. VerbaDeck works in mobile browsers but is optimized for desktop presenting.

**Q: How do I update VerbaDeck?**

A: Refresh your browser. VerbaDeck auto-updates to the latest version.

**Q: Can I export my transcript?**

A: Yes. After presenting, click "Export Transcript" to download as text file.
