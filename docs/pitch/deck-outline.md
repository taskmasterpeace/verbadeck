### Slide 2 — The problem
**Headline:** The clicker hasn't changed in twenty years. Neither has the panic.
- Mid-demo, both hands busy — you break eye contact to reach for the clicker
- A Q&A question you didn't prep for, and you freeze in front of the room
- An hour lost the night before hunting for one decent slide image
*Speaker note:* Tell one specific presenter's story — the live sales demo — don't abstract
to "the presentation industry." The pain is physical and personal.
*Visual:* A presenter fumbling a clicker / four disconnected tools. [SCREENSHOT: chaos-before]

### Slide 3 — The solution
**Headline:** The deck follows you, instead of you babysitting the deck.
- Say a trigger word → the slide advances. Hands and eyes stay on the room.
- An AI coach wraps the whole loop: trigger words, images, notes, and Q&A prep
- The insight: your voice is already the controller *and* the content — capture it once
*Speaker note:* Land the insight slowly; it's what separates this from "voice as a gimmick."
Demo the trigger-word advance live if the venue allows.
*Visual:* Voice waveform → slide transition. [SCREENSHOT: trigger-carousel]
